SHORT PEER REVIEW — "Forecasting Café Ingredient Usage from Heterogeneous Databases
with Hand-Built Exponential Smoothing"
(Garkisch / Martens)

--------------------------------------------------
(1) SUMMARY (including rating)
--------------------------------------------------
The demo forecasts daily per-ingredient usage by joining sales facts
(PostgreSQL) with recipes (MongoDB) and forecasting with two
self-implemented models: SES as baseline and additive Holt-Winters
(weekly season) as default. A CLI switches ingredients, models, and
smoothing parameters. Evaluation is strong: a 28-day hold-out backtest
with RMSE/MAE/MAPE across three ingredients (Table I), including the
honest finding that SES marginally beats Holt-Winters on the flat
coffee-grounds series. Method equations and a sound justification for
the additive variant are given; the setup section is good
(hardware, OS, DB versions, Docker Compose). Weaknesses are mostly
formal: a partly draft-state bibliography, an under-specified
recipe-to-ingredient mapping, and several language slips and imprecisions.

Rating: 3 - Weak accept

--------------------------------------------------
(2) STRONG POINTS
--------------------------------------------------
- Strong, honest empirical evaluation: 28-day hold-out with RMSE, MAE,
  and possibly MAPE across three ingredients;
  openly shows a case where the simpler baseline wins and explains
  inflated MAPE on near-zero-demand series.
- Two self-implemented models (SES + Holt-Winters) enable a live model
  comparison in the demo; additive form well justified (zero-demand
  days), equations included.
- Exemplary, reproducible setup: CPU, RAM, OS, PostgreSQL 18,
  MongoDB 8, Docker Compose, runtime indication; good literature
  context (polyglot persistence, forecasting survey).

--------------------------------------------------
(3) WEAK POINTS
--------------------------------------------------
- Recipe-to-ingredient mapping under-specified: no word on how
  espresso-shot entries or the "milk or water" choice are resolved;
  the strikingly small coffee-grounds errors (RMSE ~5 g/day) raise the
  question whether espresso-based drinks are covered — and one headline
  finding rests on that series.
- Bibliography partly in draft state: refs [1] and [2] lack authors,
  year, and details ([2] does not even name Winters although the text
  says "Winters [2]"); ref [4] prints ~80 authors verbatim over a full
  column instead of "et al.".
- Noticeable language slips (e.g. "choosen") and imprecisions (no units
  in RMSE/MAE table). MAPE is announced in the text but not in Table I.
- The described evaluation plot is not shown as a figure.
- Likely LLM-assisted but not acknowledged.
